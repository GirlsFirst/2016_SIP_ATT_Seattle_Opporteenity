INTERNSHIPS

Bellevue Art Museum Marketing Intern

Bellevue Arts Museum is seeking Marketing & PR Teenage Interns to help strengthen the museum's visibility and brand. The successful candidates will develop professional writing samples, social media management experience, and other valuable skills to jumpstart their careers.

Microsoft High School Program

The Microsoft High School internship program is one of the most rewarding programs for high schoolers available. They have the opportunity to develop cutting-edge technology, work with high-tech professionals, and get paid for their work. It is the perfect program for teens dedicated to the STEM field and ready for an intense comittment.

Environmental Stewardship

Local high schools have the opportunity to perform various tasks/projects to support Bellevue’s efforts to reduce community energy consumption and compete for the $5 million Georgetown University Energy Prize. A strong interest in environmental sustainability, clean energy, public engagement, outreach and communications is desired.

SPORTalk High School Sports Ambassador

SPORTalk is currently seeking 2 High School Ambassadors from each school in the Seattle area to join a team of Ambassadors throughout the country. SPORTalk High School Ambassadors help spread the word about SPORTalk at their schools by showing the app/site to their friends, and helping them sign up and join the Talk.

SUMMER PROGRAMS

The Salvation Army Camp Tahoma

The Salvation Army Camp partners with Camp Arnold to provide opportunities for youth in the summer on the Eastside. Their summer camps provide opportunities for growth spiritually, emotionally, and physically. It allows children to experience new activities, be in a positive and nurturing environment and experience the great outdoors.

Adventure Trek

Adventure Trek's Adventure Programs are offered during the summer, while their School Programs are offered in the fall. In addition to an immeasurable amount of fun, these programs are designed to teach leadership, teamwork, creative problem solving, self-responsibility, community living, and outdoor skills while building self-confidence.

Bellevue College Summer Teen Program

Participants may choose from a variety of different types of courses, taught in varying lengths from one week to three. The goal of the Summer Teen program is to further education and promote lifelong learning for kids ages 12-17.

Well KEPT

Well-KEPT (Kids Environmental Project Training) is a summer youth employment program offered through the Natural Resource Division of the Parks & Community Services Department. The program is designed to provide Bellevue youth with education, job skills, and career development training in the field of park resource management. Work focuses on parkland renovation and maintenance; projects may include forest enhancement, wetland restoration, and trail construction.

Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program

Girls Who Code's Summer Immersion Program's free, 7-week summer program for 10-11th grade girls who wish to learn coding and gain exposure to technology-based careers. Curriculum includes instruction on Python, Robotics, HTML, and more. Acceptance is limited and determined by application.

Bellevue College Summer Enrichment Program

The Summer Enrichment Program allows students who are 16 and 17 years old and who meet the admissions criteria to enrich their high school education by taking more challenging college level classes. These students take summer quarter classes alongside college students. They have the opportunity to earn college credit, and gain insight to the college experience.

VOLUNTEER

Bellevue Youth Theater

Anyone 13 years of age and older can volunteer at the Bellevue Youth Theater as an usher, prop master, light operator, or sound operator! This theater thrives on volunteers, and operates with the desire to provide theater arts opportunities for all young people. BYT volunteers acquire theater experience and build a strong network of supporters, while having a blast!

Parent-Teen Conflict Mediator

All teenagers have the opportunity to volunteer as conflict mediators for parents and their teenage children. The volunteers, being teenagers, tend to possess the required disposition to help teenagers settle their arguments. Volunteers must demonstrate a high level of maturity and be willing to commit a great deal of time to acquire this position, but will surely reap rewards in learning and character development by doing so.

Eastside Energy Corps

Volunteers at the Eastside Energy Corps become a part of an organization centered around helping Bellevue become more energy-efficient and green. They give informational speeches, become a part of campaigns that aim to educate the citizens of Bellevue on environmental issues facing society, and plan events that facilitate the energy conservation conversation.

Highland Center Reception Assistant

Volunteers will assist with basic reception duties including answering multi-line telephones, providing information and referrals both via telephone and in-person contact; directing participants to various classrooms; light typing, filing and making visitors feel welcome. Minimum age to participate is 16.

Kelsey Creek Farm

Teen volunteers at Kelsey Creek Farm are involved in the care and management of farm animals and ponies. Volunteers help the farm staff with daily operations, manage animal chore routines, and learn responsible equine and farm animal care. They enjoy vigorous exercise and fresh air while working outside on the farm with a variety of animals.

Bellevue Skate Park Assistant

Assistants at Bellevue Skate Park have the opportunity to help maintain the park, administer activities, and keep it running smoothly!
