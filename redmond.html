INTERNSHIPS

Teaching Internship

Craigslist is currently looking for Teachers/Instructors for their hands-on after school enrichment programs. Interns must be available between the hours of 2:00 and 5:00. This is a part time position and could be 1 to 5 days a week with approximately a 2 hr. time frame each day. After school programs take place at several elementary and pre-schools on the Eastside. This is a program for teens who enjoy working with preschool & elementary aged children, and need a part time position.

Microsoft High School Program

The Microsoft High School internship program is one of the most rewarding programs for high schoolers available. They have the opportunity to develop cutting-edge technology, work with high-tech professionals, and get paid for their work. It is the perfect program for teens dedicated to the STEM field and ready for an intense comittment.

KUOW RadioActive Youth Media

RadioActive is the youth media program at KUOW where youth delight in discovering public radio journalism. RadioActive supports young people’s growth as media makers by sharing access to public radio’s knowledge, skills and institutions. It is a short drive away from Redmond.

SUMMER PROGRAMS

Grasshopper Enrichment Education

This unique program is designed to instruct public speaking, etiquette, leadership, and other life skills not traditionally taught at schools, and not necessarily taught at home. Grasshopper instructors are professional teachers extensively trained in using the Grasshopper curriculum. They are encouraging, caring and passionate individuals who believe that all children can achieve great success.

MoveFree Parkour and Freerunning Academy

MoveFree’s week long summer programs for kids up to 16 years old are a great way to get kids active and moving over the summer. Students have fun by challenging themselves with progressive goals throughout their training, incrementally developing skills, and accomplishing objectives they may not have thought possible. MoveFree Academy also offers unique programs that fuse movie animation/computer science with parkour.

Overlake School Athletics Summer Program

The Overlake School in Redmond offers a variety of summer programs catered to different sports, including tennis, volleyball, basketball, and cross country. Teens are sure to find a program that fits their interests and allows them to stay sharp on their athletic skills over the summer.

City of Redmond Teen Summer Recreation Programs

The city of Redmond offers a variety of programs, including Stop Motion Animation Camp, Filmmaking, and Photography. Teens are sure to find a program that matches their interests!

Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program

A free, 7-week summer program for 10-11th grade girls who wish to learn coding and gain exposure to technology-based careers. Curriculum includes instruction on Python, Robotics, HTML, and more. Acceptance is limited and determined by application.

DigiPen Summer Program

ProjectFUN's Summer Programs offer exciting learning experiences for students of all ages. These courses introduce students to a variety of fun and engaging topics in video game programming, art and animation, game design, engineering, and sound and music design — all while reinforcing their knowledge of core academic subjects like math, physics, art, and writing.

TUTORS

Prep Northwest

Founded in 2004, PrepNorthwest has established itself as one of the most effective high school tutoring, SAT and ACT test preparation firms in the Greater Eastside and Seattle area. Their goal is to prodcuce results while helping students have fun.

Brightmont Academy-Redmond

Brightmont Academy is an accredited private school for grades 6 through 12 providing one-to-one instruction. One teacher works with one student to individualize learning pace, style, and goals. Courses for credit, tutoring, test preparation, and college and career assessment are also available.

Redmond Guitar Center Music Lessons

Redmond Guitar Center offers private and group lessons as well as a fun Rock Show program, where students are matched up to learn and play shows with other students at a similar skill level. Their goal is to help children get the most from their musical education.

Redmond Mathnasium

Mathnasium of Redmond has been offering math tutoring and homework help to hundreds of students in Redmond and Kirkland ever since they opened in 2008.

Allegro Music Lessons

Allegro offers music lessons for all ages on guitar, bass, piano, ukulele, violin, cello, mandolin, banjo and voice. Private lessons are taught at their studios in downtown Redmond by the top instructors.

VOLUNTEER

Gardens at Juanita Bay

The Gardens at Juanita Bay is a retirement community that welcomes residents of all nationalities and backgrounds. They were originally founded by the German Ladies Society of Seattle in 1925, but since 1937 they have been a non-profit corporation providing quality care to elder citizens of all nationalities. Just call or email them for information.

Purrfect Pals Cat Shelter at Denny’s Pet World

Volunteers in Purrfect Pal's adoption centers spend their time caring for and playing with cats, cleaning cages and acting as matchmakers for potential adopters. They have morning, afternoon and evenings shifts available seven days a week!

Redmond Old Fire House Teen Center

The Old Fire House Teen Center has been serving the teens of Redmond since 1992. It is a space for teens to get to know members of their community and have fun! They choose volunteers who want to contribute positively to their community.

Farrel-McWhirter Park Restoration

Volunteers for EarthCorps and the Green Redmond Partnership participate in environmental projects in places such as Farrel-McWhirter Park! They work to remove aggressive weeds such as reed canary grass and plant trees to help Redmond an eco-friendly area.

HopeLink

Hopelink's five centers each offer food banks, plus food deliveries to elderly and disabled individuals who are homebound. Related programs include education and seasonal activities.

Little Bit Therapeutic Riding Barn Crew

Little Bit Therapeutic Riding Center is a non-profit organization that offers a unique approach to therapy. Children, youth, and adults with physical and/or developmental disabilities from the greater Puget Sound area are enrolled in weekly therapeutic horseback riding classes that improve physical health, provide a sense of accomplishment, control, and emphasize the capabilities of each rider
